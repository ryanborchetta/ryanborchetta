Physical Product Design

Formula SAE Uprights

This is a showcase of my Fall 2019 design project for the USC Racing's 2020 Formula SAE car. I was tasked with redesigning the uprights, which are the components that link the wheel package and breaks to the suspension. The uprights are a critical component that have to be engineered to exact specifications while minimizing weight. The uprights for the 2020 car required a major redesign in order to accommodate a new 10" wheel package (down from 13" previously).

Seen here are the front (top) and rear (bottom) 2019 components. They had to be completely redesigned in order to accommodate the much smaller wheel package. This provided numerous opportunities to optimize the design in order to shave off precious weight. A direct translation of this design to a smaller package was put through finite element analysis (FEA), which revealed it was overbuilt for our expected load case.

These were the first iteration of a more streamlined design concept. They were approximately 25% lighter than the original design concept. These early versions were used to validate the fundamental design concepts under estimated loading scenario.

These are the final designs for the uprights. These versions integrated the calculate suspension mounting points as well as additional weight saving measures. They are approximately 40% lighter than the old design concept, and significantly less than half the weight of the 2019 components. Also shown are some of the FEA simulations that were used to validate the designs.
